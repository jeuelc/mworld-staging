Poles apart!

 

The poles are right at the top and right at the bottom of the Earth. The northern pole region is called the Arctic and the southern pole region is called the Antarctic. Even though both poles are covered by snow and ice they are very different places.

The Antarctic has no land mammals, no freshwater fish and very few plants. It's like an icy desert! But in spring when the ice melts whoosh!... there's a boom of tiny little plants, called phytoplankton, which attracts a huge array of animals, including migratory birds, seals and whales, all cashing in on the yummy seasonal food source!

The Arctic, on top of the world, is a frozen ocean surrounded by other land masses unlike Antarctica which is a continent surrounded by ocean! The Arctic has more places where animals like to live, from frozen seas to Scandinavian forests. The polar bear is the top predator and hunts for seals, walrus and penguins. The Arctic also supports 23 different ethnic groups of humans and they too have learnt to live and hunt on the land and ocean.

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!

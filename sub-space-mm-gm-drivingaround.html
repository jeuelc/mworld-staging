Driving around

 

The first spacecraft to successfully operate on Mars did not move. Scientists only studied the place it had landed, taking pictures, measuring temperatures, analysing the soil and air.

Since the 1990s, most of the spacecraft landing on Mars have been rovers. These are robotic cars that drive around Mars, finding new things to photograph and analyse. Rovers drive up hills and down into craters.

The biggest rover to land on Mars is Curiosity, which weighs in at 900 kg and is the size of small car. It is nuclear powered, has an array of cameras, and even a laser for vaporising and analysing rocks.

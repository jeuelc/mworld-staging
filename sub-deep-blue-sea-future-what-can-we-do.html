What can we do?

 

You and I have a very important role in protecting the world's oceans and it can be as simple as putting your rubbish in the bin!

When you go down to the beach with your family or go fishing in rivers and off piers, you can make sure that what you take; your lunch, snacks, water bottles and fishing gear, come back home with you! You can get involved with Clean-up programs with your school and community to help collect rubbish from our beaches and rivers and keep our marine life safe.

Did you know that about 50 billion water bottles are bought each year? Most of those end up in rubbish tips and they can take up to 1000 years to break down, so instead of buying more and more water bottles, you can re-use the same environmentally friendly water bottle. What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!

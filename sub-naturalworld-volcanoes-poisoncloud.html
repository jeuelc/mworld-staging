Poison Cloud

The Toba supervolcano in Indonesia erupted about 74,000 years ago. It's the world's biggest caldera, about 100 x 30 kilometres in size! The eruption was an 8 on the VEI scale. This was more than 1000 times bigger than the VEI 5 eruption of Mt Vesuvius in Italy, 79 AD, that buried the towns of Pompeii and Herculaneum.

The Toba eruption had a huge impact on the planet! It left behind volcanic deposits which we can still see today around Indonesia and in other countries, like India.

Its ash cloud was full of a poisonous gas known as sulphur dioxide, which mixed with oxygen and water in the air to form sulphuric acid (forming acid rain).

The poisonous cloud blanketed the Earth so the Sun's rays could not easily get through, like when you close your curtains on a sunny day. The Earth's temperature dropped by about 5C, possibly for years. Many plants, animals and humans died - some people estimate fewer than 10,000 humans survived!

Our Earth is small compared with other planets in our solar system. Even so, if you wanted to get to the middle you would need to dig down over 6730 kilometres!

The Earth has three main layers, the crust (what we're on), mantle (middle) and core (centre). Some rocks in the mantle and crust can get so hot they begin to melt, forming magma. This magma is lighter or less dense than the rock around it, so it rises upward. It's a bit like when you step on a muddy patch of ground and it squishes up between your toes!

The magma rises until it finds a spot where the surrounding rock is just right, not too heavy or light! It collects and forms a magma chamber. Magma can then rise to the surface by forcing its way through cracks it finds in the surrounding rock.

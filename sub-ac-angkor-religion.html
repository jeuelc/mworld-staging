To understand Angkor it's good to get an idea of the religions followed by the people of Angkor, and how these changed over time. Different Hindu gods were popular with different kings you can tell this from the inscriptions and the style of the temples the kings built. Many of the Angkorian kings worshipped the god Shiva, but others, like Suryavarman II, preferred the god Vishnu. Later, King Jayavarman VII adopted Mahayana Buddhism as his personal faith and made Buddhism the state religion of his kingdom. When Zhou Daguan visited Angkor in 1296, the main religion was Theravada Buddhism, as it is in Cambodia today. Zhou noted that not everyone was Buddhist. He saw followers of other religions, particularly Hindu traditions. Finally, there were the 'Neak Ta', the many different ancestor spirits, or guardian spirits of the land, which were worshipped in every village.

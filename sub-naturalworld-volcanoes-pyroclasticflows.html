Pyroclastic flows

 
click here

Some eruption columns that rise above volcanoes are too heavy to stay afloat in the air, so they rapidly fall to the ground. This forms large, scorching flows known as pyroclastic flows. The flows are made up of volcanic gas, pulverised magma (small bits of volcanic glass and crystals), larger magma fragments (called pumice) and any random stuff on the ground that they pick up, like trees!

Pyroclastic flows travel at hundreds of kilometres per hour, can reach around 1000°C in temperature, and travel for many kilometres! These flows are the most destructive things that can come out of a volcano. They bury, burn and boil, so you don't want to be anywhere near these bad guys!

Careful with the water!

 

essentials of life are one of the biggest challenges. We need breathable air, water and food. A litre of water weighs a kilogram, so if astronauts used water only once many tons of water would have to be launched into space.

Recycling is essential for a trip to Mars. On the International Space Station, clean water is extracted from urine (and even sweat) using a distiller. Astronaut Don Pettit has said, "Yesterday's coffee becomes today's coffee."

People breathe oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide. On the International Space Station carbon dioxide is chemically scrubbed from the air, returning oxygen in the process. On Mars, astronauts may grow their own food, using edible plants to remove carbon dioxide from the air and provide oxygen.

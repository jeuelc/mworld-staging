Taking a closer look

 

We can take a close look at Mars with the spacecraft that land on its surface. What type of rocks do we find on Mars? Did water once flow there?

The first spacecraft to successfully operate on Mars, Viking 1 and 2, revealed plains reminiscent of the Earth's deserts - a red, dusty landscape strewn with rocks. They studied the chemistry of Mars, revealing chemicals and minerals familiar to geologists on Earth, including basalt, silicon and Iron. On some mornings ice covered the ground, but this was carbon dioxide ice (dry ice) rather than water ice.

Fashion, Perfumes and Jewellry

 

Zhou Daguan said in his diary that the people of Angkor "wrap a cloth around their waists, but apart from that they leave their... chests ...uncovered". Cambodia is a very hot country so they didn't need to wear much! But the colour and design of the cloth (called a 'sampot' in Khmer) told quite a lot about a person's social rank.

For example, only senior officials and relatives of the king could wear a cloth with a scattered flower design no other men would dare to do so! These officials were often followed around by a servant holding a red or green silk parasol, or umbrella. They all wore perfumes too, like sandalwood and musk. Important women wore gold bracelets and rings and dyed their hands and the soles of their feet with henna. Some decorations show people with long ear lobes from which big heavy earrings could be hung. And did you know that all the people of Angkor had long hair and tied it in a knot?

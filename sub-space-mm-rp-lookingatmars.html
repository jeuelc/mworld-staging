Looking at Mars

 

Mars is one of the brightest planets in the sky. Unlike stars, it does not "twinkle" and its distinctive red colour distinguishes it from other planets.

Mars and all the other planets move slowly around the sky. This means we don't always see Mars at the same time of night all year round. In some parts of the world it is visible at 10pm. At other times it will be below the horizon at 10pm.

f we look at Mars through a telescope, we see that some parts are darker than others. Sometimes we can even see the polar ice caps that remind us of the poles here on Earth.

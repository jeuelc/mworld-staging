Hinding Under The Sea

Many volcanoes erupt under the sea. In fact most of the world's volcanoes lie beneath the waves! The most common type of underwater volcano is found along 'divergent' tectonic plate boundaries, that's where the plates are moving away from each other. Here hot magma rises up in the space between the plates and erupts lava. As the lava cools it forms new sea floor!

These volcanoes, known as mid-ocean ridge volcanoes, form chains and can be very long! They look a bit like the cracks that form on the top of a cake when it is cooked. The worlds longest is the Mid-Atlantic Ridge. It has lots of underwater volcanoes all along it and stretches for around 16,000 kilometres. That's like travelling from London, England, all the way to Melbourne in Australia!

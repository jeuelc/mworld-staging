How do rockets work?

 

Rockets work by burning explosive fuels, producing a jet of hot expanding gas. The rocket is pushed forward as the jet is blasted out of the rocket's nozzle.

Blast-off is one of the most dramatic and dangerous parts of space flight. The rockets accelerate rapidly, breaking the sound barrier in less than one minute. While most rockets successfully reach outer space, many never make it, having exploded soon after lift off.

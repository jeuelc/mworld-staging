Creation Myths and Legends

 

The Cambodians have a special story about how Angkor got started. In Cambodian mythology, Kaundinya, an Indian Prince, travelled to Cambodia from India long ago carrying with him a bow and arrow. When he arrived, Kaundinya shot the arrow and where it landed became the place for a new capital. Kaundinya married the princess Soma, daughter of the local Nagas king (Nagas were giant water serpents), who drank up the waters of the land and named the country Kambuja. This story explains why Khmer and Indian ways are very similar and why Khmers believe that people and nature are linked.

There's another story too. This is a legend that came from an 11th century inscription discovered just across the border in Thailand. It is more a historical legend than a myth. It tells how the Khmer king Jayavarman II crowned himself chakravartin (world emperor) in a special ceremony near Angkor in 802 CE, the date many historians mark as the beginning of the Angkorian empire. Imagine declaring yourself the ruler of the world! Who do you think you could rule over?

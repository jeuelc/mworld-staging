Not Dead Yet!

Yellowstone National Park in the USA is one of world's most stunning and well-loved places to visit. It's also one of our biggest caldera volcanoes, measuring 85 x 45 kilometres. Yep, when you walk around it you're stomping on the roof of the Yellowstone supervolcano!

It's had three major eruptions. The last one was about 640,000 years ago. So you might think it must be dead by now. Wrong. It might be getting ready to erupt again.

How do we know? If you visit the park you will see hot water and steam spurting into the air from the ground (called geysers), smoking mounds or colourful hot springs. These bits of evidence show that the Yellowstone caldera is still hot, active and ready to erupt again so be careful where you put your picnic rug!

Cracks From The Surface

The outer layer of the Earth, the crust, and the top bit of the mantle are pretty hard. They float on the lower part of the mantle, which is a little hotter and softer (but still solid!). This hotter region slowly moves in big circles, as hotter parts rise, then cool and sink, in a process known as convection. This slow convection can take many thousands of years, with the cooler and harder crust getting stretched, shoved and broken up into bits over time. These bits are known as tectonic plates.

The movement of these plates is slow, about as fast as your fingernails grow! But their movement can do big things, like form volcanoes and mountains or cause earthquakes and tsunamis!

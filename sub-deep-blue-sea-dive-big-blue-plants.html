Plants in the ocean

 

Plants in the ocean function like plants on land they fix carbon, produce oxygen and are incredibly important for lots of ocean processes. They can be all different sizes and colours; green, brown & even red!

The largest ocean plants, kelp, form forests in the ocean and provide shelter for lots of animals. You can find smaller algae on rock platforms, in rock pools and on the ocean surface. The smallest ocean plants cant even be seen with the naked eye they are called phytoplankton and are food for lots and lots of animals. When they die they sink to the bottom of the ocean and make chalk or oil.

In shallow water, you can find the only plants that can flower under water seagrasses. Dugongs and green turtles eat seagrasses, while sea creatures like prawns and fish make them their home. On the edge of oceans in the tidal muddy areas, you can find mangroves. These are specialized trees and shrubs but instead of growing their roots into the ground they grow them up into the air!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!

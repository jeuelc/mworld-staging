Ingredients for Life

 

What does life need? Oxygen, food and water. There isn't much oxygen and water on Mars, and certainly not anything we recognise as food.

However, life can be very diverse. People breathe oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide, but plants take energy from the Sun, "breathe" carbon dioxide and "exhale" oxygen. Bacteria can survive, and even thrive, on more exotic chemicals.

Most people live in areas where temperatures are between -10 and 40 degrees C, but other forms of life can handle more extreme temperatures.

We associate the Sun with life because it provides warmth and light for animals and plants. However, in some extreme places life can survive without the Sun. Bacteria can live deep underground and bizarre animals live next to volcanic vents at the bottom of the ocean.

However, one thing that seems to be essential for life on Earth is water. Is there water on Mars?

Imagining Mars

 

At the end of the 19th century astronomers began observing changes on Mars. Regions on Mars would become brighter or fainter. Was this due to changing patterns of vegetation?

Some astronomers, notably Percival Lowell, thought they could see channels on Mars - perhaps a sign of intelligent life transporting water on the Martian surface. However, many other astronomers disputed these claims, noting that the Martian air would be too thin and cold for water.

These imaginings inspired popular stories about Martian life and Martian invaders. There have been dozens of Martian films and books (even songs). The most famous story is H.G. Wells' War of the Worlds, in which alien invaders attack 19th century England.

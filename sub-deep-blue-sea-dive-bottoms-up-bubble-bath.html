Extreme bubble bath

 

At the very bottom of the ocean in some parts of the world, you will find a hot and extreme environment. This occurs where tectonic plates are slowing moving away from each other and there is hot magma close to the surface of the seafloor. Cold sea water seeps into cracks in the sea floor and is heated by the super-hot magma, creating what are called hydrothermal vents. When the water, which is full of minerals, is released out of the vents it looks like big black or white plumes of smoke, so these vents are also called chimney stacks or 'black or white smokers'.

The sea water released can get as hot as 340°C so surely nothing could survive there, right? Wrong. When these vents were first discovered in 1977, scientists found life heaps of it, such as tube-worms, crabs, fish and lots of bacteria!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!

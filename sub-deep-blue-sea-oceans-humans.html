Humans and the Ocean

Humans and the oceans go way backStone Age or prehistoric people hollowed out tree trunks to make boats called dugouts. These prehistoric boats were used for fishing and exploring. With time, humans got better at making boats, using the wind and ocean currents and then sea trade begannot to mention early exploration of the world! Other people had to manage the pottery kilns, tend the royal elephants or make sure the business of the palace was running smoothly. Imagine being appointed the official elephant minder in the kingdom, what things would you need to do?

Today over 3.5 billion people rely on the oceans for their food, with more than 70 million tonnes of fish caught each year! That's the same weight as 14 million African elephants! That's a lot of fish! But about 40% of those fish are then used to make fishmeal for feeding other fish!

What's a volcano?

 
click here click here click here

A volcano is a conduit or opening that transfers hot molten rock from inside the Earth out on to its surface. Molten or melted rock below the Earth's surface is called "magma", but when it erupts at the surface we call it "lava".

When magma gets close to the surface it can release dissolved components from the liquid that actually prefer being a gas, such as water vapour, carbon dioxide and sulphur dioxide. These gases make the magma bubbly, which builds up lots of pressure. Finally, the magma explodes through the Earth's surface as the pressure is released. It's a bit like when you shake a bottle of fizzy lemonade and then unscrew the top!

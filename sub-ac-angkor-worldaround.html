The World Around Angkor

 

Between about 800 CE and 1300 CE, the Khmer empire covered much of mainland South-East Asia. On a map, where you see Cambodia today was the heart of an empire that spread into southern areas of Thailand, Vietnam, Laos and Burma. At the crossroads between China and India, the Khmers adopted Indian religious ideas (Hinduism and Buddhism) from an early stage, even though Cambodia had more direct diplomatic and trade contact with China.

Most of Angkor's neighbours in historical times were conquered by other states and have disappeared. Among these were Champa, a kingdom on the central coast of Vietnam that Angkor often quarrelled with, the kingdoms of Dvaravati and Pegu in modern Thailand and Burma and, further away, the Srivijayan empire in Sumatra and Malaysia.

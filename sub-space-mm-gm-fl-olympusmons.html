Olympus Mons

 

The Olympus Mons is the tallest mountain in the Solar System. At 22 kilometres high, it dwarfs Mount Everest, which is just 8 kilometres high. Even when you fly in an airliner , you usually don't get higher than 12 kilometres. Olympus Mons is truly enormous.

Olympus Mons is also the largest extinct volcano in the Solar System. It was built up by many volcanic eruptions, which spread lava flows over 600 km of the Martian surface. At its top are a series of collapsed craters, spread over 70 km.

Olympus Mons is similar to the Hawaiian volcanoes of Mauna Kea and Mauna Loa. However, Olympus Mons is far bigger than its Hawaiian cousins and it last eruption may have been a billion years ago.

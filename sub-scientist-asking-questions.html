Asking questions

Asking questions is such an important part of science. In fact, some scientists think that coming up with the right questions is just as important as coming up with the answers! For example, scientists might ask questions like: "Is the sun a star?", "How can we grow more wheat on a farm?" or "Where did mountains come from?"

Good questions in science ask about things that nobody knows the answer to yet, which are important and interesting. Why are each of the above three questions important and interesting? Can you come up with three questions that are important and interesting? What is the most important and interesting question that you think scientists should solve next?

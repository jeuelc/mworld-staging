Seaside luxury!

 

Rock pools in the intertidal zone are a little like five star water hotels for the coastal creatures! They are a place where the animals and plants can stay, feed and survive underwater, while everyone else in the intertidal zone has to deal with being dried out when the tide goes out!

Rock pools are also a great place for us humans to explore! At first look, rock pools may seem like there is nothing in them but if you stay very quiet and still, you'll see heaps of life; sea stars, baby crabs and fish, sea anemones putting out their tentacles to catch food every rock pool is different and the deeper the rock pool, the more animals you could see!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!

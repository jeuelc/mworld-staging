Getting to Mars

 
 

How do we get to Mars? Even when it is closest to Earth during its solar orbit, it is still 100 million kilometres away. That is 250 times further from us than the Moon! We need a rocket and a spacecraft to travel across the vast distances between the planets.

But we are closer than we think! Robotic spacecraft have already visited Mars! What did robotic spacecraft see when they first got to Mars? Was it similar to the imaginings of astronomers from a century ago? How can we land on Mars to get a closer look?

Perhaps the first astronauts who travel to Mars will decide to take a one-way ticket. Perhaps it will be easier to live on Mars than to return home. Some people have even suggested that Mars could be a "lifeboat", keeping the human race alive if some tragedy were to happen here on Earth.

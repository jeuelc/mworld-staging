The water mystery

 

Is there water, one of the key ingredients for life, on Mars?

Even from Earth, telescopes reveal polar ice caps that grow and retreat with the seasons. Mars Phoenix spacecraft landed on the Martian North Pole in 2008. Samples studies by this robotic spacecraft revealed that the Martian poles are comprised of both frozen water and frozen carbon dioxide (dry ice).

Is there liquid water on Mars? Unfortunately, none has been found... yet.

However, spacecraft looking down on Mars have spotted dry river valleys and plains similar to those found on Earth. Spacecraft in space and rovers on the ground have also spotted layers of sedimentary rock, similar to those formed in water on Earth.

Billions of years ago, Mars may well have been a different planet. It was geologically active, like the Earth, with volcanoes and Marsquakes . It probably had a thicker atmosphere, and much of Mars may have been covered with liquid water. Was there life too?

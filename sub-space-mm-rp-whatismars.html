What is Mars?

 

ice caps, deep canyons and towering volcanoes. Its red colour is due to iron oxide (rust), which is the same reason we find red dirt here on Earth.

It is smaller than the Earth and bigger than our Moon, with a diameter of 21,200 kilometres. Being further from the Sun than the Earth, it is far colder than our home, although temperatures can climb above freezing on Martian summer days.

Mars has long fascinated us; it is so close to Earth that you can see it at night with the naked eye. You dont need a telescope to see it. Next time you are outside at night time, see if you can spot it!

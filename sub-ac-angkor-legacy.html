Gone... But is it Really?

For all Cambodians, Angkor is an important part of their heritage. You don't need to look much further than the Cambodian flag to see just how important Angkor is! Millions of people each year visit the ruins of Angkor, a source of pride and tourist income to the Cambodian people. And traces of the Khmer empire extend beyond Cambodia; many ruins are scattered through Thailand and Laos.

Some historians believe that many of the traditions of Angkorian art and culture were never really wiped out. They were adopted by the royal court in the kingdom of the Ayutthaya in Thailand after it captured the city of Angkor. In fact, the distinctive styles of Khmer dance, architecture and sculpture that developed at Angkor have been exchanged with neighboring Laos and Thailand throughout history. And they continue to be rekindled in Cambodia today!

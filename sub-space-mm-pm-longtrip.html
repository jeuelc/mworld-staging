A long trip?

 

We have yet to get people to Mars, and getting people there will be a far bigger challenge than sending robots.

Robots survive on electrical power, but we need oxygen, food and water. Any human expedition to Mars will require years of space travel, and help will be far away.

Getting people beyond the Earth requires vast amounts of equipment. The Apollo astronauts who flew to the Moon were launched on Saturn V rockets. Each of these giants was 111 metres tall and weighed in at 2800 tons! That's 5 times heavier than an A380 jumbo jet! Even a Saturn V wouldn't be big enough for a Mars mission, as expeditions to the Moon lasted only weeks, not years.

The first expeditions to Mars might use spacecraft assembled in orbit above the Earth, so several big rockets can be used rather than just one far bigger (and expensive) rocket. Once assembled, the spacecraft could use rockets to accelerate and escape the Earth's hold, and then coast for at least six months to reach Mars.

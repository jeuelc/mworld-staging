Rubbish Islands

In the North Pacific Ocean lie a few new islandsbut these islands were not created like other islands we humans have made them with all of our rubbish! These giant islands, known as the Great Pacific Garbage Patch, are more like a yucky soup where the main ingredients are plastics. They are made up of millions of tiny pieces of broken plastic, bottle tops, drink cans and even toys! These garbage patches are formed when ocean currents, wind and other ocean features come together and with them bring rubbish from all over the world.

The marine life that lives in the oceans can be harmed by our rubbish. Our tiny bits of plastic, are often confused for food and can build up in the stomachs of turtles, birds and even large whales. A giant Sperm Whale recently died and it had heaps of plastic in its tummy as well as clothes hangers, tooth paste containers, ice-cream containers and even garden pots!

It is very difficult to clean these garbage patches up but we can all help by disposing of our rubbish properly.
